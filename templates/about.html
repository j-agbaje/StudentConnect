About StudentConnect

StudentConnect is a web platform to support international students targeting students going to universities where the language barrier between English the main language of instruction and the common language of communication is particularly potent. The platform will provide features such as personalized profiles and language-based community groups. It aims to connect students and foster a more welcoming academic environment by reducing social isolation and strengthening peer support networks. For a potential broader scale application, the platform's target audience will expand to include African students from different regions of Africa and abroad, who attend African universities where English is the main language of instruction but not the language of communication. This broader scope will help address the language and cultural challenges international students face across the continent. Currently, there isn't a single, centralized platform designed to cater to these specific needs in Rwanda or throughout Africa. Although student groups and general social media platforms exist, they do not provide the specialized support that this platform would, particularly in connecting students with like-minded peers in their proximity. By focusing on the specific needs of these students, the platform will actively promote their social and academic well-being, making it a unique and essential resource for international students in Africa.

International education is expanding rapidly, with Africa witnessing an increasing number of students from diverse linguistic backgrounds. However, these students often face challenges when the language of instruction on campus is not the primary language of communication. Rwanda is one such example where the language of instruction in most of its universities is English and although a larger percentage of the population understands English to an acceptable degree, over 90% of the population speak Kinyarwanda as the language of communication and only about 0.2% speak English fluently (Chepkemoi, 2017; Eyssette, n.d.; Tour, 2021). This language barrier can lead to academic struggles, social isolation, and difficulties in forming connections, which are essential for both personal and academic growth (Beelen & Jones, 2015; Andrade, 2006).
